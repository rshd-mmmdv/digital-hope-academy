The Challenge

Why young talent
stays left behind

In Azerbaijan, children from families receiving social assistance face significant structural barriers that block access to quality digital education — and the economic independence it can bring.

Financial Constraints

High subscription and certificate fees on platforms like Coursera and Udemy are entirely out of reach for low-income families, blocking access before learning even begins.

Motivation & Language Barriers

Without local mentorship or Azerbaijani-language support, young learners struggle to stay motivated and progress independently in purely online environments.

Our Approach

A hybrid model that
bridges the gap

The cornerstone of Digital Hope Academy is a two-level hybrid mentorship system connecting world-class global content with local, human guidance in Azerbaijani.

Global Tech Curricula

Participants independently follow structured programs designed by Google, IBM, and Meta — the same content used to train professionals worldwide, made fully accessible at no cost.

Twice-Weekly Seminars

Local IT professionals host online or in-person hybrid sessions twice a week. Difficult topics are explained in Azerbaijani, and participants tackle real-world practical cases together.

Internationally Recognized Certificates

Upon completing programs, participants earn certificates from global tech giants — credentials that open doors on the international labor market, not just locally.

Freelance & Remote Pathways

Beyond learning, participants are introduced to concrete freelance and remote work opportunities — enabling them to generate income and support their families during the program.

Expected Impact

What changes after
Digital Hope Academy

By equipping young people with real digital skills and income potential, the project works to permanently break the cycle of intergenerational poverty in Azerbaijan.

Financial Independence

Families reduce dependence on state social assistance as graduates begin earning in the digital economy.

Global Competitiveness

Skills aligned with leading tech company standards open doors far beyond the local labor market.

Problem-Solving Skills

Practical, mentor-guided experience builds independently working professionals with strong analytical abilities.

Social Justice

Making technology accessible to low-income families actively strengthens equality of opportunity across society.

Social Integration

Internationally recognized certificates help participants feel like full, valued members of modern society.

Risk Assessment

Identified challenges
we are managing

Changing TSSA Eligibility Status

Since the project targets children from families receiving Targeted State Social Assistance, a family's eligibility status may change or be discontinued during the program period. Removing a participant mid-program risks interrupting their educational journey at a critical stage of skill and confidence development.
